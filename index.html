WELCOME TO INTRAMUROS

WHERE YOU CAN GO

Casa Manila

A step back in time to the Spanish colonial era, Casa Manila is a beautifully restored 19th-century mansion showcasing the opulent lifestyle of the elite during the Spanish period. Wander through intricately furnished rooms and charming courtyards that reflect the grandeur of a bygone era.

Manila Cathedral

An iconic landmark of the Intramuros district, Manila Cathedral stands as a symbol of faith and resilience. With its grand architecture, intricate carvings, and stunning stained-glass windows, this neo-Romanesque cathedral is a must-see for history enthusiasts and spiritual seekers alike.

Baluarte de San Diego

Explore the remnants of one of the oldest stone fortifications in Manila, Baluarte de San Diego. With its lush gardens and ancient walls, this historic bastion offers a tranquil escape and a glimpse into the military history that once protected the city.

THE BEAUTY OF INTRAMUROS

WHY YOU SHOULD VISIT INTRAMUROS

Glimpse of History

Intramuros is a living testament to Manila's Spanish colonial past, offering visitors a glimpse into the Philippines' rich history. With landmarks like Casa Manila, you can explore traditional colonial architecture and lifestyle.

Immerse in Culture

The walled city's cobblestone streets and historic charm transport you to another era. Walking through Intramuros feels like stepping back in time, surrounded by well-preserved structures like the iconic Manila Cathedral.

See Iconic Landmarks

For those seeking tranquility, Baluarte de San Diego provides lush gardens and peaceful views. It's a perfect spot to relax and reflect amid the historical surroundings.
